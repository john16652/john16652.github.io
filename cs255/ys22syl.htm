Objectives

The objectives of this course include the following:

  • experience learning a programming language "on your own" as is commonly the case in industry.
  • understand the syntax and semantics of the Python language
  • understand how to develop and implement a program in the Python language
  • understand various forms of data representation and structures supported by the Python language
  • understand the appropriate applications of the Python language
  • recognize similarities and common characteristics of programming languages.

The student should develop or enhance skills in the following areas:

  • proficient programming in the Python language and programming in general
  • design and revision of Python scripts
  • debugging techniques appropriate for the Python language

Class structure

This is a self paced course with no formally schedule class meetings. Success in this course depends highly on your self discipline. Office hours are to be used to ask questions and clarifications. Individual  meetings with the instructor may be required to go over work in progress.

Reading

The reading will consist of chapters from the online ebook and from the Python documentation site. Since you should have had programming experiences prior to this course and that this material assumes no such background, the reading should be relatively easy.

Assignments

The student is expected to do the reading as specified below prior to developing the associated program.  There may be a choice of program for each week's assignment. The student is expected to submit the assignment by the due date (see exceptions in the assignment descriptions) of each week via Moodle. The "how to submit" is described below.  The instructor will review the program by compiling and running the program with his own data set and may reply with suggestions for improvements.  If warranted, the assignment may be revised and resubmitted.  See grading below.

Comments and suggestions regarding each assignment will be provided on each submission.  It will be your responsibility to review the comments and possibly make the corrections suggested for resubmission.

Help

Being a self-paced course, some concepts may not be easily grasped from the readings. The instructor is available during office hours for individual help on these matters. Please use the office hours for clarification. Be sure that you have identified at least one office hour per week that you can regularly come for questions and have programs reviewed. We may be able to do most of the discussion and program review by email. E-mail is also an ideal and preferred communication medium for short questions or clarifications.

Grading

A variant of the "portfolio method", analogous to the writing portfolios used in College Writing Seminar, will be used in this course. Students are required to submit programs to the instructor on a regular basis for review and critique according to the syllabus. The instructor will review your programs electronically with written critiques through Moodle. It is your responsibility to understand the critiques and either resubmit the project or apply the comments to future projects.

The first submission of each completed and documented program must be received by the instructor no later than due date specified in Moodle. They must compile cleanly and be a viable solution to the problem. Programs may then be revised to include the suggestions given from the review and resubmitted.

There are 100 points in total for course assignments. Most projects are worth 10 points. A few at the beginning of the course are worth less. If the submission is acceptable you get all the points for the project. If it is late, then there will be points deducted. If the project has problems, it will be given a fairly low grade (e.g., 3 or 4 out of 10) but you will be given another week from the review to resubmit for full credit. On the second review, the project may receive a quality score of 10 or less, but no chance for a third submission.

There is a 1 point deduction from the final grade for each day late per program. If the program does not compile, nor is appropriately documented, nor is a reasonable solution to the problem, the program will be rejected as a submission, and the late penalty could apply. This is to discourage junk being sent in to stop the clock.

Python Portfolio Grading

The final grade will be assigned based on the completeness, quality of the final programs in the portfolio, demonstrated improvement in programming skills over the course, timeliness of program submissions, attendance to the requested individual office meetings, and the reflection paper.

At the end of the course you must write a 2-3 page reflection paper describing what you learned about Python and the process of learning a language on your own.

All materials for the course, submissions of programs and reflection paper, and all due dates are in Moodle.

Course policies

These standard course policies are described on this web page. Please read them carefully, especially on academic integrity. Exceptions to my standard policies are given below.

Attendance

Does not apply, but you must submit the programs on a regular basis.

Late program policy

Late first submissions will have a negative effect on your final grade. Typically 1% per school day late is deducted from your final grade. So a program 10 school days (2 weeks) late could reduce the final grade by a whole letter grade. However, extensions may be given in warranted situations that are agreed upon at least one day prior to the due date by instructor and student.

Please note that a program submitted which

  • does not compile,
  • is not a viable solution to the problem, or
  • is not documented

will be rejected as a submission and therefore considered late. 

Program submission details

  • Develop, implement and test each program.
  • Be sure each program is well documented, with good choice of variable names and use of standard Python style.
  • Submit the documented source file of the program (do not send the object or executable files) using Moodle.
  • See the instructor for any clarification prior to submission.
  • We will use Moodle to keep track of the progress and comments for all assignments.
  • If the review comments are minor, they will simply be posted in Moodle. If more substantial discussion of a program is necessary, an office meeting will be called.
  • Read the comments carefully and if a revision is explicitly requested, you must resubmit the revision within a week of the review, again using Moodle.
